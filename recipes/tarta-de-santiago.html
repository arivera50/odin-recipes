Tarta de Santiago

Description

Tarta de Santiago is a traditional almond cake from Galicia, Spain, linked to the Camino de Santiago pilgrimage. Made with ground almonds, eggs, and sugar, it has a moist texture and a rich, nutty flavor. Naturally gluten-free, itâ€™s often flavored with lemon zest, cinnamon, or a touch of liqueur.

Its signature look comes from a powdered sugar topping with the cross of Saint James stenciled in the center. Served as a dessert or with coffee, the cake is a proud symbol of Galician culture and a favorite among pilgrims and locals alike.

Ingredients

  • 2 and 1/2 cups of almond flour
  • 1 cup of white sugar
  • 5 large eggs
  • 1 tsp of cinnamon
  • 1tbsp of vanilla extract
  • peel of 2 lemons
  • 2 tbsp of confectioners' sugar

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350F/180C. Line an 8 inch/20cm round springform/cake pan/tin with parchment on the bottom.
  2. Mix the almond flour/ground almonds, lemon zest and cinnamon and mix until combined.
  3. Add eggs one by one, do not over mix.
  4. Add vanilla extract.
  5. Pour mixture into baking pan. Bake for 30 minutes.
